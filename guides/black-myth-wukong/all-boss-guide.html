Pro Tip: The Wandering Wight is a massive skill check disguised as an early-game optional boss. He teaches you the game's fundamental rhythm: Immobilize → charged heavy → dodge → repeat. Don't skip him — if you can beat the Wight at level 8, you have the skills to handle everything through Chapter 3. Target his left leg (his stagger point). After 4 charged heavies to the same leg, he falls over for a free critical hit window.

Warning: Do NOT fight the Yellow Wind Sage without the Wind Tamer vessel (obtained from the secret area, Kingdom of Sahali). Without it, his whirlwind phase is nearly impossible — the sandstorm reduces visibility to 10% and he heals 5% HP per second while inside it. With the Wind Tamer, you dispel the sandstorm instantly. This is the game's way of teaching you: explore secret areas BEFORE fighting chapter final bosses. Every chapter has a secret area with a vessel that counters the final boss.

Pro Tip: Yellowbrow's gimmick is that he nullifies ALL spells for the first 90 seconds of the fight. Most players panic and die. The counter: use Spell Binder (obtained from the secret Xu Dog quest in Chapter 3) BEFORE the fight. Spell Binder disables all spells but boosts your attack by 80% — since Yellowbrow disables spells anyway, this is pure upside. With Spell Binder active and a fully upgraded Jingubang, you can melt his first phase in under 40 seconds.

Warning: Erlang Shen is the hardest boss in the entire game — harder than the final boss by a significant margin. Three phases, 45,000 total HP, summons celestial dogs, and has a phase 3 transformation where he becomes a giant celestial warrior that one-shots you with grab attacks. Recommended: level 80+, fully upgraded Jingubang, Samadhi Fire transformation, and at least 5 Celestial Pills. Most players need 20-40 attempts. The reward is the True Ending — and it's absolutely worth it.

True Ending Requirements

  1. Complete ALL secret areas in Chapters 1-5 (5 total: Ancient Guanyin Temple, Kingdom of Sahali, Bitter Lake, Purple Cloud Mountain, Bishamon Temple).
  2. Defeat all 6 Loong bosses (Red, Black, Cyan, Yellow, White, Golden).
  3. Defeat Erlang Shen at Mount Huaguo (unlocks the "Third Eye" relic).
  4. Fight the final boss (Great Sage's Broken Shell) with the Third Eye relic equipped.
  5. During the final boss Phase 2, refuse his offer to "return to the mountain."
  6. The True Ending shows the Destined One's true identity and sets up the planned DLC expansion.

Pro Tip: Red Tides is the best overall transformation through the entire game — not because it's the strongest (Samadhi Fire outclasses it in pure damage), but because it has the fastest light attack animation and the lowest Might cost. You can use Red Tides 3-4 times per boss fight, compared to 1-2 times for heavier transformations. In a game where transformation uptime is everything, Red Tides wins.
