Getting Started: Your First Minutes

The Gothic 1 Remake drops you into the Valley of Mines — a massive open-air penal colony surrounded by an impassable magical barrier. You're a nameless prisoner with nothing but rags and a vague letter of introduction. The game does not hold your hand, and that's the point.

Your first objective should be to reach the Old Camp, the largest settlement in the Valley. Follow the dirt path from the exchange point, but stay alert — the wilderness is dangerous and creatures will kill you in two hits at this stage.

Pro Tip: Before entering the Old Camp gates, talk to the guard at the entrance. He'll give you a basic rundown of camp rules. More importantly, he marks your map — something you'll rely on constantly.

Once inside the Old Camp, your first five tasks should be:

  • Find Diego near the campfire in the outer ring — he's your first real contact and provides starting gear.
  • Learn the layout — the Old Camp has three rings: the outer ring (peasants and new arrivals), the middle ring (guards and merchants), and the inner castle (Ore Barons).
  • Scavenge everything — rusty swords, old coins, mushrooms, herbs. Everything has value in Gothic.
  • Talk to every named NPC — Gothic's quest design relies on conversation. If someone has a name, they have something useful to say.
  • Do NOT pick fights — at level 1, even a juvenile scavenger can end your run. Respect the food chain.

Understanding the Three Factions

Gothic's faction system is the core of the game. You must eventually join one of three camps, and your choice fundamentally changes your entire playthrough — different quests, gear, trainers, and storyline branches.

FactionLocationPlaystyleDifficulty
Old CampCentral ValleyWarrior / Melee FocusedEasy
New CampSouthern Rice FieldsHybrid / MercenaryMedium
Swamp CampEastern SwampsMage / Alchemy FocusHard

The Old Camp is the recommended starting faction for beginners. It has the most accessible quests, the best early trainers, and a straightforward progression path. The New Camp offers more freedom and better access to water magic, while the Swamp Camp is for players who want the deepest magic system at the cost of a brutal early game.

Warning: Once you commit to a faction in Chapter 2, you CANNOT switch. The other camps become permanently hostile if you've progressed far enough in your chosen faction's questline. Make a hard save before committing.

Combat Fundamentals

The remake modernizes Gothic's famously quirky combat while preserving its deliberate, weighty feel. Here's what you need to know:

  • Lock-on is now toggleable (Tab on PC, R3 on controller). Use it for 1v1 fights; turn it off for groups.
  • Directional attacks matter — side swings hit wider arcs, overheads deal more damage to single targets. The game reads your movement input during the attack windup.
  • Blocking costs stamina but can be held indefinitely. Perfect blocks (timing within 8 frames) stagger enemies and refund stamina.
  • Dodging has i-frames now (new to the remake). Backstep and sidestep are viable defensive options.
  • Weapon proficiency gates your moveset — you literally cannot combo with a weapon you lack the skill for. Train before equipping.
Pro Tip: The single most important early skill is One-Handed Weapons Level 1, which costs 10 LP from any combat trainer. Without it, your attack animations are deliberately sluggish and your damage is halved. This is the game's way of telling you: train or die.

Essential Early Quests

Here are the quests you should prioritize in your first 5 hours, regardless of which faction you're eventually joining:

  1. Diego's Welcome — escorts you through the Old Camp and teaches basic systems. Rewards: 50 XP, a map, and a rusty sword.
  2. Whistler's Blade — a simple fetch quest from the smith that teaches the barter system. Rewards: 100 XP, 30 ore.
  3. The Missing Hunter — your first real combat encounter. Teaches tracking and enemy awareness. Rewards: 200 XP, hunter's bow.
  4. Mordrag's Errand — introduces you to the New Camp and cross-faction politics. Rewards: 150 XP, faction reputation.
  5. Shadows in the Mine — first dungeon dive, teaches light mechanics and environmental hazards. Rewards: 300 XP, first magic ring.

Gear & Progression Path

Gothic's progression is resource-gated, not level-gated. You can theoretically sequence-break to endgame gear if you know where to look and can survive the trip. Here's the recommended progression for beginners:

Pro Survival Tips

  • Sleep to save and heal — beds are your save points and full-heal stations. Learn where every bed in every camp is.
  • Cooking is NOT optional — raw meat heals 5 HP. Cooked meat heals 40 HP. Find a pan, learn to cook.
  • Night is dangerous — predators become more aggressive and some enemies only spawn after dark. Travel during daylight when possible.
  • NPCs have schedules — a blacksmith won't train you at midnight. Learn NPC routines and plan your visits.
  • Ore is everything — it's currency, crafting material, and sometimes quest items. Never sell ore unless absolutely necessary.
  • Climb everything — the remake's climbing system lets you reach rooftops, cliffs, and hidden ledges. Many secrets are vertical.
Final Tip: Gothic rewards patience. If an area feels too hard, it probably is. Go explore somewhere else, complete side quests, train your skills, and come back stronger. The Valley of Mines is open from the start — use that freedom.
