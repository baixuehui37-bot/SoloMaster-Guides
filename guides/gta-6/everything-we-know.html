Total estimated map area: 4.5-5× the size of GTA V's Los Santos and Blaine County combined. This makes it Rockstar's biggest open world by a significant margin, exceeding even Red Dead Redemption 2's map in navigable playable area (though RDR2's felt larger due to horse travel speed).

Pro Tip: The most significant change from GTA V is verticality. Trailer 2 shows fully enterable skyscrapers in downtown Vice City — not just lobbies, but 40+ floors of interiors. Combined with the new "Parkour 2.0" movement system (ledge grabbing, wall running, window entry), the effective playable space in the urban core is roughly 3× larger than the raw square footage suggests.

Warning: The Hurricane system is not just visual. A Category 3+ hurricane disables aircraft, floods low-lying areas (Vice Beach becomes impassable), and causes NPCs to evacuate. If you have an active mission in a flood zone, it becomes unavailable until the storm passes (2-4 in-game days). Plan your mission routing around weather forecasts accessible via the in-game phone.

Trailer Breakdown & Hidden Details

Trailer 1 (December 2025): Set the tone. Vice City at sunset. Lucia in prison. The gator in the convenience store. "Trust" whispered at the end. Key hidden detail: the GPS coordinates shown on the police cruiser's dashboard (25.774°N, 80.191°W) point to a real Miami address — the old Rockstar office. Fan service or lore hint? Still debated.

Trailer 2 (April 2026): Action-focused. Showed the dual-protagonist gameplay, vehicle customization, and the hurricane system for the first time. Mario Ernesto Sánchez (voice of Lucia) confirmed as lead. Key hidden detail: The yacht scene at 2:34 shows "1985" on a champagne bottle — the year of the original Vice City's setting. Timeline theorists suggest flashback missions are possible.

FAQ: What We Still Don't Know

  • Is there a first-person mode? Not confirmed. GTA V and RDR2 both had it, so it's very likely, but Rockstar hasn't shown it yet.
  • Will there be story DLC? Unknown. Rockstar's post-launch focus for GTA V was entirely on Online. A story expansion would be a major shift in strategy.
  • Cross-play between PS5 and Xbox? Not confirmed. GTA Online 2 will support cross-progression (your account transfers between platforms), but cross-play hasn't been announced.
  • Can I kill either protagonist? The relationship system and "trust" mechanics strongly suggest branching endings, including potentially a betrayal/revenge path. But Rockstar hasn't confirmed if one or both protagonists can permanently die.
  • How long is the story? Estimated 60-80 hours for the main story alone (per previews that cite "significantly larger" than GTA V's 32-hour main story). With side content, 150+ hours.
