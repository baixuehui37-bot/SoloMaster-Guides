Samurai vs Ninja: When to Switch Forms

Nioh 3's defining innovation is the dual-form system. At any moment, you can instantly swap between Samurai and Ninja styles — and mastering when to switch is the difference between 50 deaths and a clean run.

AspectSamurai FormNinja Form
PlaystyleDefensive, methodical, trading blowsAggressive, hit-and-run, speed kills
Block Effectiveness90% damage reduction, high stability50% damage reduction, block breaks in 2 hits
DodgeMedium roll (11 i-frames)Quick dash (14 i-frames, shorter recovery)
Ki RecoveryKi Pulse restores 40% more KiDodging costs 30% less Ki
Damage per HitHigh (good for slow, armored enemies)Lower per hit, higher DPS on fast targets
Burst Counter TypeBrute (interrupt attack)Feral (dodge-counter)
Best vsBosses, Yokai, armored humansTrash mobs, fast enemies, human duelists
Pro Tip: Switch forms during enemy red "Burst Attacks." If you're in Samurai form (Brute counter), you smash through their attack. In Ninja form (Feral counter), you phase-dodge and leave an afterimage. Form-switching is instant and has zero animation lock — you can switch mid-combo between the second and third swing. The game tracks which form you're in when the Burst Attack connects, not when it starts winding up.

Ki Pulse: The Single Most Important Mechanic

If you take nothing else from this guide, take this: learn to Ki Pulse on every single attack string. Without it, you're playing at 40% efficiency.

Ki Pulse TimingEffectDifficulty
White Pulse (standard)Recover 60% of spent Ki. Purify nearby Yokai poolsEasy (tap R1/RB when blue particles gather at your chest)
Perfect Pulse (frame-perfect)Recover 100% of spent Ki + 15% bonus. Purify larger area. Activates Flux bonusMedium (8-frame window at the peak of particle convergence)
Flux (stance switch during pulse)Recover extra 20% Ki. Chain directly into different stance attacksHard (press R1 + stance button simultaneously during Perfect Pulse window)
Flux II (double stance switch)Recover extra 40% Ki. The ultimate technique for infinite combosVery Hard (R1 + stance1 + stance2 in 12 frames)
Pro Tip: The Ki Pulse timing window varies by weapon. Fast weapons (katana, dual swords, tonfa) have a 45-frame window. Heavy weapons (axe, odachi) have a 55-frame window but the animation is slower, so the real-world timing feels similar. Practice with the katana first — it's the most forgiving. Go to the Dojo training ground and practice Perfect Pulses for 10 minutes before you start the game. You'll die 50% less.

Burst Counter Guide: Brute, Feral, Phantom

Burst Counters are your answer to the red-flash attacks that would otherwise one-shot you. Each Guardian Spirit type gives a different Burst Counter — and choosing the right one for your playstyle is the most important character creation decision.

TypeInputEffectDifficultyBest For
BruteR2+Circle (interrupt)Smash forward, interrupting the enemy's attack. Works ANYTIME during the red flash, even if the attack hasn't startedEasyBeginners, aggressive players
FeralR2+Circle (dodge)Quick dodge that leaves an afterimage. You must time it so the enemy attack HITS the afterimage, not you. Narrow windowMediumMobile players, Ninja form users
PhantomR2+Circle (block)Stationary shield that parries the attack on contact. Tightest timing but safest on successHardParry experts, Samurai form tanks
Warning: Your Guardian Spirit determines your Burst Counter type PERMANENTLY for that loadout. You can change Guardian Spirits at shrines, but each spirit is locked to one type. If you pick a Phantom spirit and can't land the timing, you'll suffer. Start with a Brute spirit (Kato, the starting fire wolf) — it's the most forgiving and works against 95% of Burst Attacks.

Stance Switching: Flowchart & Combos

Nioh 3 retains the three-stance system (High/Mid/Low) from previous games. But in this entry, stance switching during Flux generates bonus Ki, making it an offensive tool, not just a defensive one.

StanceDamageSpeedKi CostBest Use
High★★★★★★★Boss punishes, horn breaking, slow enemies
Mid★★★★★★General combat, blocking, crowd control
Low★★★★★Fast enemies, status application, dodge-focused fights

Optimal combo route for katana: Low stance (gap close) → Mid stance (2-3 quick hits) → Flux to High (heavy finisher) → Ki Pulse → dodge out. This loop costs zero net Ki with Perfect Pulses and deals 30% more damage than staying in one stance.

Top 10 Beginner Mistakes in Nioh 3

  1. Ignoring Ki Pulse. This is the #1 cause of death. Without Ki Pulse, you run out of stamina mid-fight and get guard-broken. Every. Single. Time.
  2. Staying in one form. The dual-form system is not optional. Enemies have attacks that are MUCH easier to counter in one form vs the other. Switch constantly.
  3. Hoarding items. Use your Amrita crystals to level up. Use your elixirs. Use your talismans. The game showers you with consumables — they're worthless in your inventory.
  4. Heavy armor without stats. Wearing heavy armor without enough Stamina puts you above 70% equip load = "C Agility" = your dodge becomes useless. Stay under 70% at all costs.
  5. Ignoring the Blacksmith. Disassemble gear, forge new weapons, and temper stats. A +3 weapon with the right stats does 3x more damage than an unmodified +5.
  6. Not using ranged weapons. Headshot a human enemy with a bow before the fight starts = instant kill. The bow is not optional — it's your opener on every human encounter.
  7. Skipping Kodama. Each Kodama you find increases your elixir drop rate. Find all 25 in a region and you start every life with 8 elixirs instead of 3.
  8. Not purifying Yokai pools. Enemies regenerate in Yokai pools. Ki Pulse near a pool to purify it. If you don't, the fight never ends.
  9. Fighting everything. You don't have to kill every enemy. Run past trash mobs to the boss. The runback is part of the game's design — use it.
  10. Giving up after 5 deaths. Yamagata Masakage (first boss) kills most players 10-15 times. This is normal. You're learning. Each death teaches you one more attack pattern.

First Boss Strategy: Yamagata Masakage (The Tutorial Wall)

Yamagata Masakage is designed to test everything you've learned in the tutorial. He's fast, aggressive, and has a Burst Attack that will kill you if you haven't practiced your counter.

AttackTellCounter
4-Hit ComboWeapon glows white, 2-second windupBlock first 3 hits in Samurai form, dodge 4th, punish with 2 High stance hits
Burst Attack (red flash, spinning charge)Red aura, weapon spins above head, 1.5-second windupBrute Counter immediately (you can interrupt during windup). After counter, he's staggered for 4 seconds — use Ninja form for a full combo
Jumping SlamCrouches, then vertical leapDodge sideways (not backward — the AoE is wide). Punish during 3-second recovery
GrappleLeft hand reaches forward, no weapon glowDodge backward twice. Unblockable. If grabbed, mash dodge to escape earlier
Phase 2: Fire Sword (50% HP)Sword ignites, arena dimsSwitch to Ninja form. His attacks are now faster but each swing costs HIM Ki. Dodge 5-6 hits in a row and he'll exhaust himself — free punish window
Pro Tip: The boss arena has a small shrine in the back-left corner. If you die, you respawn AT the shrine, not at the beginning of the level. This makes Yamagata Masakage one of the most generous runbacks in the game. Use this to practice his patterns without the frustration of a 3-minute corpse run. Most players beat him on attempt 8-12.
