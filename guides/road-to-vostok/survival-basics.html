Before You Go: Stash & Loadout

Your first raid in Road to Vostok sets the tone for every run after. The game's permadeath system means everything you bring can be lost permanently — but going in with nothing means your survival odds are roughly 8%. Here's the optimal starter loadout:

Enemy Patrol Patterns & AI Behavior

Road to Vostok's AI is unusually sophisticated for a solo-dev game. Scavs have three behavioral states:

  • Idle (60% of time): Standing, smoking, looting bodies, talking. Easy headshots if you spot them first.
  • Alert (25% of time): Heard a sound, investigating. Move erratically, check corners. Much harder to ambush.
  • Aggressive (15% of time): Engaged in combat. Push your last known position, flank in groups of 2+, use grenades.

Warning: Never loot in the open. The #1 cause of death for new players is standing still over a body. Loot from crouch, spin 180° every 2 seconds, and listen for footsteps. If you hear sprinting, abandon the body immediately — a PMC heard your shots and is pushing you.

Extraction Timing & Safe Routes

Road to Vostok's extraction system punishes greed. Each raid has a 40-minute timer. Extractions are not always open — some require keys, others have conditional triggers (power on, flare gun).

Pro Tip: Always extract with at least 8 minutes remaining. Scav players spawn in at the 15-minute mark and specifically hunt players heading to extracts. The last 10 minutes of every raid belong to player scavs — don't be there.

10 Common Mistakes That Get You Killed

  1. Running everywhere. Sound travels 120m for sprinting, 40m for walking, 0m for crouch-walking. Walk unless you're in open terrain with no cover.
  2. Fighting every scav you see. Every gunshot broadcasts your location to every player on the map. Kill quietly (melee, suppressed pistol) or don't kill at all.
  3. Looting without clearing the area first. Clear the room, wait 30 seconds for anyone who heard the fight, THEN loot.
  4. Using the same route twice. Veterans memorize common paths. If you entered through Farmstead, extract through Crossroads, not back through Farmstead.
  5. Ignoring hydration. At 50% hydration, your screen blurs. At 25%, you make noise when moving. At 0%, you take damage. Drink before you're thirsty.
  6. Not bringing grenades. A single F-1 grenade (8,000 ₽) can clear a room of 3 scavs. It's the best ROI item in the game.
  7. Engaging at long range with iron sights. If you can't see them clearly, they can't see you either — reposition instead of taking the 15% accuracy potshot.
  8. Standing near windows. Silhouette is the #1 way snipers spot you. Stay 2m back from windows, peek from the side, not the center.
  9. Extracting too late. The 5-minute warning is NOT a cue to start heading to extract. You should already be there.
  10. Gear fear. The Makarov in your stash is worthless if you never use it. Bring your best gear. You'll find more.
