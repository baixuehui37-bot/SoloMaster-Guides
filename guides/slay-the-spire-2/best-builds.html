Necromancer: Minion Swarm & Soul Harvest

The Necromancer is the most flexible new character in Slay the Spire 2, capable of generating up to 8 minions simultaneously. The key to mastering this class is understanding that minions are not just damage dealers — they are shields, energy generators, and combo enablers.

Warning: The Necromancer's minions count as "creatures," not "cards played." They do NOT trigger Shuriken, Kunai, or Ornamental Fan. Build your relic strategy around this limitation.

Paladin: Divine Shield & Smite Control

The Paladin is the safest class for climbing Ascension. With the highest base HP (85) and the Divine Shield mechanic, you can face-tank hits that would obliterate other characters.

S-Tier: Divine Shield Stacking

CardRarityRoleCopies
Holy BulwarkCommonGain 8 Block. If you already have Block, gain 12 instead3-4
Divine FavorUncommonGain 1 Divine Shield stack (negates ALL damage from next hit)2-3
Righteous FuryRareDeal damage equal to your current Block × 21
ConsecrationRareAll enemies lose 3 Strength. Heal 15 HP1
SmiteCommonDeal 9 damage. If target is debuffed, deal 18 instead2

The Divine Shield build stacks Block to 40-60 per turn, uses Righteous Fury for 80-120 damage nukes, and maintains threat immunity with Divine Favor stacks. Consecration strips enemy Strength scaling — essential for the Act 3 boss gauntlet.

Best Relics for Paladin

  • Holy Grail (Boss): Start each combat with 1 Divine Shield. This relic alone raises your win rate by 20% on A20.
  • Calipers (Shop): Retain 15 Block between turns. Synergy with Righteous Fury is broken.
  • Fossilized Helix (Event): Negate the first 2 hits each combat. Effectively 2 free turns.
  • Tungsten Rod (Rare): Reduce ALL incoming damage by 1. With 40+ Block per turn, this makes chip damage irrelevant.
Pro Tip: The Paladin's starting relic (Crusader's Vow) heals 2 HP after every combat. Combined with the Vampiric Charm event relic, you effectively heal 6 HP per floor. On A20, this is the difference between dying on floor 48 and beating the Heart at full HP.

Act-by-Act Strategy Guide

Act 1: Prioritize front-loaded damage. All three Act 1 elites (Gremlin Nob, Lagavulin, Sentries) punish slow decks. You need at least 2 attack cards upgraded by floor 6. Take 3 hallway fights minimum before your first elite — you need the card rewards.

Act 2: This is where most runs die. Act 2 enemies scale fast (Snake Plant, Snecko, Avocado). You need either a scaling damage engine (poison, strength, Dark orbs) OR AoE clear by floor 25. Byrds on floor 18 will kill you if you have no multi-hit attacks. The Act 2 boss (Collector, Bronze Automaton, Champ) tests your scaling — if you can't output 100+ damage by turn 5, you're dead.

Act 3: The Woke Bloke and Time Eater are the run-enders. Build specifically for these fights once you know your Act 3 boss. Awakened One punishes power-heavy decks. Time Eater punishes infinite combos. Donu & Deca test AoE damage. Adjust your final 5 floors accordingly.

Act 4 (The Heart): The Heart attacks on turns 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9 for 60-70 damage per multi-hit. You need either 60+ Block per turn, intangible, or a turn-4 kill setup. Beat of Death deals 1 (A0-A9), 2 (A10-A19), or 3 (A20) damage per card played — the Rogue suffers hardest here.
