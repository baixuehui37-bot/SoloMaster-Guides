Your First 30 Minutes

Solarpunk drops you onto a small floating island with nothing but a basic multitool and the clothes on your back. The sky stretches infinitely in every direction, dotted with islands of varying sizes. Your first instinct might be to explore — resist it. The first 30 minutes should be purely about securing your starting island.

Here's your minute-by-minute priority list:

MinutesPriorityWhat to Do
0-5SurveyWalk the entire perimeter of your starting island. Mark every resource node you see. Note wind direction (shown by grass movement).
5-10Gather WoodPunch the small driftwood trees (3 hits each). Collect 20+ wood. Don't bother with large trees yet.
10-15Craft Workbench10 wood + 5 plant fiber = Portable Workbench. This is your most important early item.
15-20Craft Basic ToolsWooden Pickaxe (8 wood), Wooden Axe (6 wood + 3 stone). Now you can actually gather efficiently.
20-25Gather Stone + FiberUse your pickaxe on the grey rock outcroppings. Collect 30+ stone and 20+ plant fiber.
25-30Build Shelter Frame15 wood + 10 stone = Basic Shelter Frame. Place it in the center of your island.
Warning: Do NOT jump off your starting island to explore others until you have a glider (requires 45 minutes minimum of progression). Without one, you cannot return. Islands are at different altitudes — gravity is permanent.

Crafting Your First Tools & Shelter

The crafting system in Solarpunk is progression-gated — you unlock recipes by building the corresponding workstation. Here's the optimal crafting order for your first hour:

  1. Portable Workbench (10 wood + 5 fiber) — Unlocks all basic tool recipes. Build this FIRST.
  2. Wooden Pickaxe (8 wood) — Allows stone gathering. Without this, you're stuck.
  3. Wooden Axe (6 wood + 3 stone) — Triples wood gathering speed over punching.
  4. Basic Shelter Frame (15 wood + 10 stone) — Provides a save point and bed. You will die without this.
  5. Campfire (5 wood + 8 stone) — Cooking station. Raw food is 3x less effective.
  6. Storage Chest (12 wood + 4 fiber) — 24 slots. Your inventory has only 15 slots total.
  7. Furnace (20 stone + 10 clay) — Smelts ore into ingots. Gates all mid-game progression.
Pro Tip: Place your workbench, campfire, and storage chest within 5 meters of each other. When all three are adjacent, you get the "Organized Workspace" buff — crafting speed +25% and resource costs reduced by 10%.

Solar Power Basics

Power is everything in Solarpunk. Your first solar panel is a major milestone that unlocks automated crafting, powered tools, and — critically — the airship docking station.

Your first solar setup needs exactly:

  • 1 Basic Solar Panel — 8 copper ingots + 4 quartz crystals + 2 iron ingots
  • 1 Basic Battery — 5 copper ingots + 10 stone + 3 plant fiber
  • 4 Copper Wires — 1 copper ingot each (craft at workbench)
  • 1 Power Controller — 2 iron ingots + 4 copper wire + 1 quartz

Placement matters enormously. Solar panels generate power based on direct sunlight exposure, which varies by altitude and time of day. For maximum output:

  • Place panels on the highest point of your island
  • Orient them facing the sunrise direction (east, marked by the permanent dawn glow)
  • Keep panels clear of shadows from trees, buildings, or terrain — even partial shade cuts output by 40%
  • Panel efficiency at different altitudes: Sea level = 60%, Mid-altitude = 85%, High altitude = 100%

Navigating Between Floating Islands

Once you have your glider (crafted from 6 cloud vines + 4 wood + 2 copper ingots), the entire sky opens up. But island-hopping is more dangerous than it looks.

Glider Mechanics:

  • Hold jump mid-air to deploy (Space on PC, A on controller). You fall at 60% normal speed.
  • Pitch down (forward) to trade altitude for speed — up to 3x horizontal speed.
  • Pitch up (back) to trade speed for altitude — briefly gain height at the cost of momentum.
  • Your glider has 120 durability. Each second of flight costs 1 durability. It breaks mid-air if it hits 0.
  • Wind direction (shown by the wind sock on your UI compass) affects your trajectory. Flying against the wind doubles durability drain.
Warning: Always carry a spare glider. If your glider breaks mid-flight between islands, you fall. There is no recovery. The death penalty is losing all items in your inventory (equipped gear is safe).

Surviving Your First Night

Night in Solarpunk isn't just darker — it's genuinely dangerous. Nocturnal creatures spawn, temperatures drop, and solar power stops generating. Here's your night survival checklist:

  1. Be inside your shelter before sundown (the sky turns orange — you have about 3 minutes of in-game time).
  2. Light your campfire. This keeps nocturnal skulkers away (they're scared of fire) and prevents the "Cold" debuff.
  3. Cook all your raw food. Night is the perfect time — cooking takes 30 seconds per item and you can queue up to 10 items.
  4. Organize your storage. Use the night to sort inventory, plan tomorrow's goals, and craft non-powered items.
  5. Sleep for 8 hours. Sleeping heals you to full, saves the game, and skips to dawn. If you have a bed, there's no reason to stay awake at night early on.

15 Pro Tips for Solarpunk Beginners

  1. Wind direction matters for everything — flying, sailing, even crop growth. Always check your wind sock.
  2. Clouds are resources. Fly through rain clouds with a collection net to gather water crystals. Fly through storm clouds at your own risk (lightning).
  3. Don't expand your island too early. Island expansion costs 50 wood + 30 stone per tile. Wait until you have automated wood/stone farming.
  4. Befriend the Skywhales. These giant peaceful creatures migrate between island clusters. Following them leads to rare resources and hidden islands.
  5. Automate everything possible. The Auto-Harvester (unlocked at Furnace + Power Controller) is the single most important mid-game item.
  6. Build vertically. You can stack platforms upward. A 5-story tower gives you a massive gliding range boost.
  7. Keep one of everything. The crafting tree is deep and unpredictable. That "useless" glowing mushroom might be the key ingredient for your airship engine.
  8. Rain irrigates crops automatically. Don't waste water crystals on irrigation if you build your farm on an island that gets regular rainfall.
  9. Enemies have elemental weaknesses. Storm Wasps hate fire, Rock Golems are weak to water, and Shadow Bats flee from light.
  10. Multiplayer is co-op only and makes everything easier. Resource costs don't scale with player count. Bring a friend.
  11. Your first airship should be small and fast. A 2x3 deck with basic sails is better than a slow 5x5 fortress. Speed = access to more resources.
  12. Island seeds are shareable. Found an amazing starting island? The seed code is in the pause menu (F3 on PC). Share it with the community.
  13. Dismantle, don't destroy. You get 80% of materials back when dismantling (hold E). Breaking things with tools only returns 30%.
  14. Keep your island lightweight. Every structure adds weight. Heavy islands drift downward slowly over days. Build a Lift Engine before expanding too much.
  15. The day/night cycle is 48 minutes real-time (24 min day, 12 min dusk, 12 min night). Plan your expeditions accordingly.
Final Pro Tip: Solarpunk's world is procedurally generated but follows rules. Island clusters at similar altitudes share biome types. If you need copper (mid-altitude), fly horizontally, not vertically. The fastest progression comes from targeting specific altitude bands, not random exploration.
